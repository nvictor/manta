PITCH

The persuasion sequence: Pain revelation, intuitive solution, trap of convention, conviction statement, hard proof.

Why Most Pitches Stall

People rarely resist because they do not understand your idea.

They do not feel the pain

The problem sounds abstract, optional, or delayed.

They trust the default

Existing habits feel safer than a proposed change.

They need evidence

Energy rises when the case moves from opinion to proof.

The PITCH Formula

P Pain revelation
I Intuitive solution
T Trap of convention
C Conviction statement
H Hard proof

1. Pain Revelation

Start by making the cost of the current state obvious and concrete.

What to do

Name the friction, waste, delay, risk, or missed upside the audience already lives with.

What it changes

The audience stops hearing a proposal and starts recognizing a problem that already belongs to them.

2. Intuitive Solution

Present the answer in a way that feels like the natural response to the pain.

Make it legible

Explain the idea in plain terms before adding mechanics, features, or implementation detail.

Reduce cognitive load

The audience should think, “of course,” not “I need a diagram to follow this.”

3. Trap of Convention

Show why the current default keeps producing the same bad outcome.

Habit

We do it this way because it is familiar.

Cost

That familiarity hides repeated losses, delays, or failure modes.

Release

The pitch becomes an escape from a bad loop rather than a risky leap.

4. Conviction Statement

State the claim cleanly and without hedging.

Say what you believe

Make one direct argument about what should happen next and why.

Give the room direction

Conviction organizes attention. It tells listeners what all the previous slides were building toward.

5. Hard Proof

Finish with evidence that survives scrutiny.

Data

Numbers, benchmarks, or measured outcomes.

Demonstration

A visible example, prototype, pilot, or before-and-after contrast.

Social validation

Case studies, references, adoption signals, or expert endorsement.

Rule: End on proof, not explanation. The audience should leave with something they can repeat and defend.

The Sequence In One Line

Pain makes the audience care. Intuition makes the answer feel obvious. Convention exposes the bad default. Conviction gives direction. Proof makes belief durable.

Use PITCH When You Need Commitment

Good fits

Fundraising, strategy proposals, product launches, sales narratives, change management, and public talks.

Audience test

If people need to change their mind, not just absorb information, use the full sequence.
