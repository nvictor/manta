PARC

The four design principles: Proximity, Alignment, Repetition, Contrast.

Why PARC Works

Good design is usually not about adding more.

It organizes attention

The viewer quickly sees what belongs together and what matters most.

It reduces friction

The layout feels intentional instead of improvised.

It makes choices teachable

PARC gives you a practical way to critique and improve almost any page or slide.

The PARC Sequence

P Proximity
A Alignment
R Repetition
C Contrast

1. Proximity

Put related things near each other. Separate unrelated things clearly.

What to do

Cluster labels with their content, keep headings close to the material they introduce, and create visible gaps between different groups.

What it changes

The viewer stops guessing relationships. Structure becomes visible before they read every word.

2. Alignment

Every element should look intentionally placed, not casually dropped.

What to do

Snap text, images, captions, and cards to shared edges or columns. Let invisible lines govern the page.

What it changes

Alignment creates calm. It makes even dense information feel trustworthy and easy to scan.

3. Repetition

Reuse visual signals so the design feels coherent rather than accidental.

Type

Keep heading styles, body styles, and emphasis patterns consistent.

Shape

Reuse spacing, card treatments, icon sizing, and corner radii.

Color

Repeat accent colors with purpose so they become meaningful instead of decorative noise.

4. Contrast

Differences should be clear enough to communicate hierarchy immediately.

What to do

Use real shifts in size, weight, color, spacing, or shape. If two things are different, make them noticeably different.

What it changes

Contrast tells the eye where to start, what to skim, and what deserves emphasis.

Common Failure Pattern

Most weak layouts do not fail from one bad choice. They fail from mild disorder in all four principles.

P

Everything is equally spaced, so relationships are unclear.

A

Objects almost line up, but not quite.

R

Styles drift from one section to the next.

C

Hierarchy is too weak to guide attention.

Fast PARC Checklist

Proximity

Can I tell what belongs together without reading every line?

Alignment

Do the major elements share edges, columns, or a visible grid?

Repetition

Are recurring elements styled the same way across the design?

Contrast

Is the hierarchy obvious in under three seconds?
