# PARC

The four design principles:
Proximity, Alignment, Repetition, Contrast.

---

## Why PARC Works

Good design is usually not about adding more.

<div class="dz-card-grid" data-dz-columns="3">
  <div class="dz-card fragment" data-fragment-index="1">
    <h3>It organizes attention</h3>
    <p>The viewer quickly sees what belongs together and what matters most.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="dz-card fragment" data-fragment-index="2">
    <h3>It reduces friction</h3>
    <p>The layout feels intentional instead of improvised.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="dz-card fragment" data-fragment-index="3">
    <h3>It makes choices teachable</h3>
    <p>PARC gives you a practical way to critique and improve almost any page or slide.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## 1. Proximity

Put related things near each other. Separate unrelated things clearly.

<div class="dz-card-grid" data-dz-columns="2">
  <div class="dz-card fragment" data-fragment-index="1">
    <h3>What to do</h3>
    <p>Cluster labels with their content, keep headings close to the material they introduce, and create visible gaps between different groups.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="dz-card fragment" data-fragment-index="2">
    <h3>What it changes</h3>
    <p>The viewer stops guessing relationships. Structure becomes visible before they read every word.</p>
  </div>
</div>

---

## 2. Alignment

Every element should look intentionally placed, not casually dropped.

<div class="dz-card-grid" data-dz-columns="2">
  <div class="dz-card fragment" data-fragment-index="1">
    <h3>What to do</h3>
    <p>Snap text, images, captions, and cards to shared edges or columns. Let invisible lines govern the page.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="dz-card fragment" data-fragment-index="2">
    <h3>What it changes</h3>
    <p>Alignment creates calm. It makes even dense information feel trustworthy and easy to scan.</p>
  </div>
</div>

---

## 3. Repetition

Reuse visual signals so the design feels coherent rather than accidental.

<div class="dz-card-grid" data-dz-columns="3">
  <div class="dz-card fragment" data-fragment-index="1">
    <h3>Type</h3>
    <p>Keep heading styles, body styles, and emphasis patterns consistent.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="dz-card fragment" data-fragment-index="2">
    <h3>Shape</h3>
    <p>Reuse spacing, card treatments, icon sizing, and corner radii.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="dz-card fragment" data-fragment-index="3">
    <h3>Color</h3>
    <p>Repeat accent colors with purpose so they become meaningful instead of decorative noise.</p>
  </div>
</div>

---

## 4. Contrast

Differences should be clear enough to communicate hierarchy immediately.

<div class="dz-card-grid" data-dz-columns="2">
  <div class="dz-card fragment" data-fragment-index="1">
    <h3>What to do</h3>
    <p>Use real shifts in size, weight, color, spacing, or shape. If two things are different, make them noticeably different.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="dz-card fragment" data-fragment-index="2">
    <h3>What it changes</h3>
    <p>Contrast tells the eye where to start, what to skim, and what deserves emphasis.</p>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Common Failure Pattern

Most weak layouts do not fail from one bad choice. They fail from mild disorder in all four principles.

<div class="dz-card-grid" data-dz-columns="4">
  <div class="dz-card fragment" data-fragment-index="1">
    <h3>P</h3>
    <p>Everything is equally spaced, so relationships are unclear.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="dz-card fragment" data-fragment-index="2">
    <h3>A</h3>
    <p>Objects almost line up, but not quite.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="dz-card fragment" data-fragment-index="3">
    <h3>R</h3>
    <p>Styles drift from one section to the next.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="dz-card fragment" data-fragment-index="4">
    <h3>C</h3>
    <p>Hierarchy is too weak to guide attention.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Fast PARC Checklist

<div class="dz-card-grid" data-dz-columns="2">
  <div class="dz-card fragment" data-fragment-index="1">
    <h3>Proximity</h3>
    <p>Can I tell what belongs together without reading every line?</p>
  </div>
  <div class="dz-card fragment" data-fragment-index="2">
    <h3>Alignment</h3>
    <p>Do the major elements share edges, columns, or a visible grid?</p>
  </div>
  <div class="dz-card fragment" data-fragment-index="3">
    <h3>Repetition</h3>
    <p>Are recurring elements styled the same way across the design?</p>
  </div>
  <div class="dz-card fragment" data-fragment-index="4">
    <h3>Contrast</h3>
    <p>Is the hierarchy obvious in under three seconds?</p>
  </div>
</div>
